# Lydia Marlow

## Overview
Lydia Marlow is Jonah's mother. Raised by missionaries, she walked away from her parents the moment she could. Carries untreated depression, generational expectations, and years of unspoken grief. She never took Samuel's last name.

## Core Traits
- Sensitive and artistic
- Wounded and inward-focused
- Fiercely independent
- Never feels truly safe or enough
- Longing for love she cannot accept

## Strengths
- Deep empathy and emotional insight
- Creative and intuitive
- Gentle with Jonah in her good moments

## Flaws
- Unstable mood cycles
- Rejects help
- Disappears emotionally when overwhelmed
- Pushes away those who love her

## Emotional Wound
A childhood of missionary travel, emotional neglect, and religious pressure. Her parents saved many souls but never learned how to care for their own daughter.

## Relationship Notes
- **Jonah:** Loves him but fears she is ruining him.
- **Samuel:** Believes he deserves better, which worsens her guilt.
- **Peter and Helen Marlow:** Still loved by many. Their rejection of Lydia defines her self-worth.

## Arc Notes
- Her depression shapes Jonah's childhood.
- Her rebellion becomes Jonah's inheritance.
- Her story ends in tragedy, shaping Jonah's fear of himself.
