# Samuel Hale

## Overview
Samuel Hale is Jonah's father. A hull worker who maintains and repairs the ship's outer shell. Humble, steady, faithful, and defined by quiet resilience. He carries the scars of a violent father but never passes that harm forward.

## Core Traits
- Gentle and patient
- Physically strong, emotionally grounded
- Deep but private faith in God
- Hard worker with a simple worldview
- Protective of family
- Never raises his voice

## Strengths
- Emotional stability
- Reliability
- Integrity
- Practical intelligence and hands-on skills
- Capable of deep forgiveness

## Flaws
- Avoids confrontation to a fault
- Holds too much inside
- Refuses help even when needed
- Believes he must be the strong one always

## Emotional Wound
Being the oldest son of a violent alcoholic. He learned to protect others and hide his own pain. He vowed to be nothing like Gregory Hale.

## Relationship Notes
- **Jonah:** Loves him more than anything. Sees Jonah's storms before Jonah admits they exist.
- **Lydia Marlow:** Loves her with patience. Watches her depression deepen and feels helpless.
- **Gregory Hale:** Sees the good in him that no one else remembers.
- **Ruth Hale:** Inherits her quiet faith and steady morals.

## Arc Notes
- Stays a moral center in the story.
- Never wavers in his belief in Jonah.
- His gentle faith shapes Jonah's journey even after Samuel is gone.
